I've always struggled to use Twitter. For some reason I become super anxious and
uneasy about posting anything. Maybe it's impostor syndrome? Why would anyone be
interested in what I have to say? Or maybe it's that I don't want people I knew
in high school to see me vulnerable. Either way, I have to force myself to use
it and am always less happy after I scroll through the timeline.

Lately I've been inspired by the book _[Share Your
Work!](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/20874659-show-your-work)_ by Austin
Kleon as well as the [learn in
public](https://www.swyx.io/writing/learn-in-public/) movement by
[Shawn](https://twitter.com/swyx) and [digital
gardens](https://tomcritchlow.com/blogchains/digital-gardens/) by
[Tom](https://twitter.com/tomcritchlow). I wanted a space where I can post
free form thoughts and technical problems I am working on without having to
worry about making it a fully polished blog post. This is typically what social
media is for, but for me, this is what this page is for.
